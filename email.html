2015 GridIron DevOps Hackathon

An Internal Booz Allen Hackathon

 

SIG Digital invites you to participate the our GridIron DevOps hackathon where you will learn and practice the DevOps methodology by using automated tools to achieve continuous integration and deployment of an existing application.

 

 

By participating in the hackathon, you will be helping us develop DevOps intellectual capital! You will be able to strengthen your DevOps skills and get experience in this increasingly important and in–demand discipline. Help set the groundwork for Booz Allen (and by extension, yourself) to be acknowledged by leaders in the DevOps community.

Challenge Overview

You will be tasked with challenges to show off your understanding of DevOps and applying these concepts as a team. For each challenge, your team will be awarded points upon successful demonstration of your solution.

This hackathon is not to see who can build the best application, but to see who can achieve the most effective DevOps solution to support an existing application. Each team will be provided with a preconfigured AWS environment that has many of the components of the application in–place. The focus will be on integrating undeveloped pieces of this environment into a full DevOps solution.

Exercises

The exercises for this hackathon are grouped according to the CAMS DevOps methodology, which stands for Culture, Automation, Measurement, and Sharing. Each exercise will have instructions to complete and will also include a few open–ended questions to help teams expand on the solution for differentiation.

Judging

You will be observed throughout the Hackathon by DevOps experts for each of the CAMS principles.

At the conclusion of the Hackathon, each team will present their final product to a panel comprised of the five experts.

Submissions will be scored at the end of the competition on the following criteria:

    Culture: (2 exercises, 20 points)

  • Open Source Code Management (2 exercises, 20 points)

    • Automation: (5 exercises, 50 points)

  • Infrastructure–As–Code (3 exercises, 30 points)
  • Continuous Deployment (2 exercises, 20 points)

    • Monitoring: (2 exercises, 20 points)

  • Dashboard / reporting (1 exercise, 10 points)
  • Unified Logging (1 exercise, 10 points)

    • Sharing: (1 exercise, 10 points)

  • Communication / Chap Ops (1 exercise, 10 points)

The panel of judges will review each of the submissions and average their scores for each team. Judges will be evaluating solutions based on the following criteria:

Applied Concepts: 20%

Innovative Approach: 20%

Playbooks and Plays: 20%

Technical Documentation: 20%

Scalable Approach: 20%

Rules

Fresh Code Rule

All code developed must be FRESH–that's how we keep everything fair. Before the start of the GridIron DevOps Hackathon, participates must create a playbook for Reliability or Automated Testing for example. Instructions on how to create a playbook will be provided.

Who Owns the IP?

The participants with the best playbook will be have it published on GridIron GitHub page with attribution. The winner for Hackathon will have their product presented as a case study for the GridIron initiative.
